Why

Signal Gap Mesh provides an alternative model of communication that prioritizes decentralization, openness, privacy, and community empowerment.

Resilient Communication

Disasters often damage or overload centralized communication infrastructure, such as cell towers and data centers. Mesh networks, with their decentralized and self-healing nature, can continue to function even if individual nodes are affected. This ensures that critical communication remains operational, enabling disaster response teams to coordinate effectively.

Community Building

Since this essentially functions as a group text message chain, we hope people will use the network to build a community. Share information about your band's performance tonight, engage in discussions about local politics, ask for a good cheese dip recipe, and more.

Resist Censorship

The network resists censorship because there's no central authority with the ability to control or restrict the flow of information.

Openness and Transparency

It's built using open-source protocols and software, allowing anyone to inspect the code, understand how the network operates, and contribute to its improvement.

Privacy and Security

Participants can send encrypted messages to each other, enhancing the privacy and security of communication within the network.

Scalability

The network can be easily expanded by adding more nodes, without requiring major changes to the existing infrastructure.

Affordability

The network runs on affordable and low-power devices, making it cost-effective, especially in areas where traditional network infrastructure is difficult or expensive to deploy.
